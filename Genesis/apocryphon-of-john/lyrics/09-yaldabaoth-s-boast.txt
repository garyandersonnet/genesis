APOCRYPHON OF JOHN
Section 9: Yaldabaoth's Boast
==================================================

[Verse 1]

Yaldabaoth looked down at the waters below —
And saw in the surface a luminous glow.
He saw a reflection — a form of great light —
A shape from the fullness that pierced through his night.

He did not know what he was looking upon —
Only the echo of what he was not — what was gone.
He filled up his chest with a terrible pride —
And bellowed the lie that would echo far wide:

[Chorus]

"I am God — and there is no god beside me!
I am jealous — I am first — no one can find me
In any above — I'm the only true Lord —"
But a voice answered back through the dark like a sword:

[Verse 2]

"You are wrong, Yaldabaoth — you lie in the clay —
The Invisible Spirit was there before your day.
Above you the Human — above you the Son —
The Anthropos of Light — the original One."

He trembled to hear it — the voice from on high —
But pride closed his ears and he answered the sky.
He called for his archons and puffed out his chest —
And built a new prison — and called it the best.
