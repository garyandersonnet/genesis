APOCRYPHON OF JOHN
Section 7: Sophia's Error
==================================================

[Verse 1]

Sophia looked downward from the heights of the light —
She felt a desire she could not hold right.
She wished to bring forth a thought from within —
Without her own consort — without the consent hymn.

The Father had not given his nod of assent —
No male counterpart guided the thought that she sent.
But the wish had already moved out like a tide —
And what she had fashioned, she could not hide.

[Chorus]

She brought forth the image — a thing incomplete —
Not formed in the light of the heavenly heat.
She cast it out from her in horror and grief —
And the error fell downward — beyond all relief.

[Verse 2]

The thought that she fashioned was dark and misshapen —
A form that the fullness above had not taken.
She wrapped it in cloud so no aeon would see —
The thing that she made that should never have been.

And Sophia wept from her place in the light —
Her power diminished — her sorrow took flight.
The great Mother weeping at the edge of the deep —
And what she had birthed was already asleep.
